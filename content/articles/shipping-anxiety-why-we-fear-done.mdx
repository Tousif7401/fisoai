You've been working on this project for months. It's *almost* ready.

Just one more feature. Just one more refactor. Just one more polish pass.

Deep down, you know: **you're avoiding shipping.**

## The Fear Behind the Delay

Shipping anxiety usually stems from one of these:

- **"What if it's not good enough?"** — Perfectionism in disguise
- **"What if nobody cares?"** — Fear of irrelevance
- **"What if they find bugs?"** — Fear of judgment
- **"What if this is all I've got?"** — Fear of running out of ideas

Here's the uncomfortable truth: **the fear doesn't go away by waiting.**

## Perfectionism Is a Trap

Perfectionism feels like quality standards, but it's often actually:

- **Procrastination** — "I'm still working on it" sounds better than "I'm afraid to finish"
- **Protection** — As long as it's not shipped, it can't be critiqued
- **Stalling** — Avoiding the next (unknown) thing

The perfect version doesn't exist. The shipped version does.

## What "Good Enough" Actually Means

"Good enough" isn't about mediocrity. It's about:

- ✅ Core functionality works
- ✅ Critical bugs are addressed
- ✅ It provides value to someone

Everything else can come later. That's what updates are for.

**v1 is supposed to be v1** — not v10 in disguise.

## The Reality Check

When you finally ship:

1. **Most people won't notice** the "flaws" that kept you up at night
2. **Some will love it** in ways you didn't expect
3. **Criticism will hurt** but won't kill you
4. **You'll ship again** — and it'll feel easier

The anticipation is almost always worse than the reality.

## Practical Steps to Ship

### 1. Set a Ship Date (and Tell Someone)
Public commitments create accountability. Pick a date and announce it.

### 2. Define "Done" Before You Start
Write down what "finished" means before you begin. When you hit those criteria, stop.

### 3. Create a "Later" List
Every time you think "just this one more thing," write it down for v1.1 instead of doing it now.

### 4. Ship Small
The bigger the release, the higher the stakes feel. Ship incrementally.

## After You Ship

Do these three things:
1. **Celebrate** — You finished something. That matters.
2. **Rest** — Take a break before starting the next thing.
3. **Reflect** — What did you learn? What would you do differently?

Then start the next thing.

## The Bigger Lesson

Your worth isn't tied to any single project's reception.

You've shipped before. You'll ship again. Each time, it gets a little easier.

**The fear doesn't go away — you just learn to ship anyway.**
